Why Do We Even Try?

A Conversation on Effort, Struggle and the Purpose of Life

Monday, March 11th at 7 PM | Maryland 110

RSVP

We find ourselves continuously striving towards personal goals and ambitions, often at the cost of our own personal well-being. But why? Why do we work so hard for the things we think we want? What motivations do we have and are they the right ones? Moreover, even if we do have the right motives, we can find ourselves frustrated, exhausted, and even hopeless. What do we do then? Why should we try even after countless setbacks and shortcomings?

This year, we plan to host a forum between Lydia Dugdale, physician and ethicist at Columbia University and Anand Pandian, Professor of Anthropology, as they discuss and share insights from their respective fields and worldviews about the question "Why do we try?" We are all faced with this question in one form or another, and no matter what we do to ignore or suppress it, it resurfaces.

Join us on Monday, March 11th, 7 PM in Maryland 110 for this important conversation. Reception with food to follow.

This event is part of a larger organization of university dialogues called Veritas Forum. Started at Harvard in 1992, Veritas Forums are campus-wide events that partner with university students and scholars to foster dialogue across worldviews to explore the ideas that shape our lives.
